## The Partnership Angle

Pick **one** to lead with based on how the conversation flows. Don't pitch all three.

**Option A — Data sharing** *(best fit if they talk about organizer relationships)*
They have early access to tournament info from organizers. You want intel before it goes public.
Offer in return: list their court/coaching as a featured partner on mytournamentpb.com.

**Option B — Cross-promotion** *(best fit if they talk about content or audience)*

"This week's open tournaments near you" drops. Their reach, your data.

**Option C — Just explore** *(if it genuinely feels too early)*

## If They Ask You to Fix Their Calendar Website

High chance this comes up. Here's the honest answer:

> "I can take a look at it — I build things with AI so I can usually figure out what's going on. But I want to be upfront: I'm not a traditional developer. I can't be your on-call tech person or commit to ongoing maintenance. What I can do is use AI tools to investigate and fix specific problems. If it's something that needs regular support, you'd want someone proper for that."

If they push and want you to look anyway:
> "Sure, show me — I'll tell you what I think the issue is. No promises until I see it."

This is the right answer because:
- Honest (protects you from being stuck supporting something)
- Still genuinely helpful (you probably can fix a one-off problem)
- Sets correct expectations upfront

## Questions to Ask Them

Ask these to make it a real conversation, not a pitch:

1. **"How do you currently find out about new tournaments? Organizers come to you, or you have to chase?"**
2. **"The calendar on your site — did you build that or is it a plugin?"**
3. **"What does your audience actually use you for most — courts, coaching, news?"**
4. **"Are you trying to grow ThePickleBase into a full business, or is it still mostly passion project?"** *(read the room first)*
5. **"Who else in the Malaysia pickleball space do you think is doing interesting things?"**

## Closing the Meeting

End with something concrete, even if small:

> "This has been really useful. Let me think about what would make the most sense to do together. Can I follow up this week with a specific idea?"

Gets you out with a clear next step without committing to anything on the spot.
